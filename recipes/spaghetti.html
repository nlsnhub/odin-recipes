Spaghetti & Tomato Sauce

Description

Spaghetti with ground beef tomato sauce is a classic Italian-American dish that combines long, thin pasta with a savory, hearty sauce. The sauce is made by cooking ground beef until browned, then simmering it with tomatoes, garlic, onions, and a blend of herbs like basil and oregano. The result is a rich, flavorful sauce that clings to the spaghetti, providing a satisfying and comforting meal. The dish is typically garnished with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil or parsley, enhancing its flavor and presentation. The combination of the tender pasta with the meaty, tomato-based sauce creates a harmonious blend of textures and tastes, making it a popular and enduring choice for family dinners and casual gatherings.

Steps

  1. Boil a large pot of salted water and cook the spaghetti according to package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, cook ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  3. Add chopped onions and minced garlic to the skillet with the beef, cooking until softened. Stir in tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, along with herbs (like basil and oregano), salt, and pepper. Simmer for about 15-20 minutes.
  4. Toss the cooked spaghetti with the meat sauce in the skillet, ensuring the pasta is evenly coated with the sauce.
  5. Serve the spaghetti hot, topped with grated Parmesan cheese and fresh basil or parsley if desired.
